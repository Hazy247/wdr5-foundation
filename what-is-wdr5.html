What is WDR5?

A clear starting point for families, clinicians and anyone learning about WDR5-related neurodevelopmental conditions.

About the WDR5 gene

WDR5 is a gene that provides instructions for a protein involved in regulating how other genes are switched on and off. Changes affecting this gene can disrupt typical development and may be associated with a range of neurodevelopmental features.

Because WDR5-related conditions are rare, knowledge is still developing. Every person is different, and the combination and intensity of features can vary considerably.

Features families may encounter

  • Developmental delay or intellectual disability
  • Differences in speech, language and communication
  • Motor, coordination or muscle-tone differences
  • Feeding, sleep, sensory or behavioral challenges
  • Medical features that require individual specialist review

This information is educational and should not replace advice from a qualified healthcare professional who knows the individual.

Diagnosis and next steps

A diagnosis is usually made through genetic testing, often after an assessment by a clinical genetics or neurodevelopmental team. After diagnosis, families may be offered further medical checks, therapy referrals and ongoing developmental support based on individual needs.
